Astitva Archive

A structured scholarly system for the study of knowledge, existence, and consciousness.

Overview

The Astitva Archive is an integrated scholarly system developed for the rigorous study of foundational questions concerning existence, knowledge, and consciousness. It brings into dialogue classical intellectual traditions—particularly the Veda—with contemporary scientific and philosophical inquiry.

Structure of the System

Svādhyāya Saṁhitā (SVS) → Research in formation

Astitva Archive (AST) → Stabilized knowledge

The archive operates through a two-layer epistemic structure: exploratory research notes are developed within the Svādhyāya Saṁhitā, and mature insights are subsequently formalized into stable scholarly works within the Astitva Archive.

Cycle of Inquiry

Astitva → Vedic-Hṛdaya-Cakṣu → Svagarbhā → Astitva

Inquiry begins with the question of existence (astitva), proceeds through interpretive vision, and culminates in generative principles, before returning to the question of being itself.

Research Programme

The archive supports long-term research into ontology, cosmology, cognition, and the structure of knowledge systems, with particular attention to Vedic and Indian philosophical traditions.
